2. Options and Derivatives Risk

Options involve risk and are not suitable for all investors. You can lose all of your investment and, in some strategies, more than your initial investment.

Options and derivatives are complex instruments. Risks include, but are not limited to:

  • Total loss: Option buyers may lose the entire premium paid, plus commissions and fees.
  • Unlimited or substantial loss: Option writers and certain spread, ratio, uncovered, short, margin, or assignment-sensitive strategies may face losses greater than expected, including theoretically unlimited losses.
  • Assignment and exercise risk: Short options may be assigned at any time when assignment is permitted, including before expiration, around dividends, during volatility, or when liquidity is poor.
  • Expiration, pin, and gap risk: Positions may change rapidly near expiration, after hours, or around market-moving events. Small price moves can materially change outcomes.
  • Liquidity and execution risk: Bid-ask spreads, volume, open interest, trading halts, market closures, and broker restrictions can prevent or impair entry, adjustment, or exit.
  • Volatility and time decay: Option values may change due to implied volatility, time decay, rates, dividends, borrow costs, and model assumptions, even when the underlying price moves as expected.
  • Margin risk: Margin requirements can change without notice. You may receive margin calls, be forced to deposit funds, or have positions liquidated at unfavorable prices.
  • Concentration risk: Repeated trades in one ticker, sector, expiration, strategy, or account can magnify losses.
  • Tax and regulatory risk: Option strategies can have complex tax, wash-sale, straddle, constructive-sale, reporting, and regulatory consequences.

Before trading options, review the current options disclosure document, broker risk disclosures, margin agreements, and applicable exchange or regulatory materials. Consult qualified professionals where appropriate.
