## Why This Market Is Actually Good for Premium Sellers

If you've been watching your portfolio bleed this quarter, here's the silver lining: **elevated implied volatility means fatter premiums**. When fear drives IV higher, options sellers get paid more to take on risk — and right now, several quality names are sitting at historically high IVR readings while their underlying businesses remain fundamentally sound.

I ran a quantitative analysis across seven tickers on my radar — **INTU, UBER, ADBE, CMCSA, OKTA, QLYS, and DXC** — scoring each across four dimensions: options liquidity, fundamental quality, IV suitability, and strategy compatibility. Here's what I found, and which names made it onto my April watchlist for new positions.

---

## The Scoring Framework

Each ticker was scored out of 10 across four weighted categories:

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Options liquidity** | 3 pts | Open interest, bid/ask spreads, weekly availability |
| **Fundamentals** | 3 pts | FCF, moat, profitability, balance sheet |
| **IV suitability** | 2 pts | IVR vs. own history, beta-adjusted premium |
| **Strategy compatibility** | 2 pts | How many strategies are realistically viable |

Higher score = more strategies available, better risk/reward, more confidence in assignment.

At ~$400, it's sitting below both its 50d and 200d moving averages, which means elevated IVR (~60%) and juicy premium relative to its own history.

**Key metrics:**
- **Market cap:** $115B · **FCF:** ~$3.5B · **Beta:** 0.56
- **IV estimate:** 32–38% · **IVR estimate:** ~60%
- **52-week range:** $310–$540 · **Earnings:** May 21 ⚠️
- **Options liquidity:** Excellent (weekly + monthly + liquid LEAPS)

**What makes it special:** Every strategy on the board works here — CSP, Wheel, Covered Call, PMCC, and Put Spreads. That flexibility is rare. Low beta means assignment won't destroy you if the market drops another 10%.

### #6 — QLYS (Qualys Inc) · Score: 5.5/10

QLYS has the **best fundamental quality in cybersecurity on this list** — 45.7% operating margin, 59% YoY FCF growth, near-zero debt. The catch is thin options liquidity (~300K average daily volume). Bid/ask spreads run $0.50–1.00+ wide, which means you'll give up significant edge on fills.

**Rule:** Always use limit orders at the midpoint. Monthly expirations only. Keep position size small.

---

### ❌ DXC — Do Not Trade

DXC Technology scores 2.5/10 for one simple reason: **extreme IV on a distressed stock is not an opportunity, it's a warning.**

- Down ~84% from its all-time high of $92
- Declining revenue, ongoing restructuring, D/E ratio of 1.80
- Options: poor liquidity, wide spreads, minimal open interest
- If assigned at any strike, the stock could continue falling well below your cost basis

**The rule of thumb:** When IV is extreme because the underlying is in structural distress, you're not getting paid for volatility — you're getting paid to take on credit risk. Skip this one entirely.
