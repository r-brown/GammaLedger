Selling Premium Into the Selloff: April Options Watchlist

Why This Market Is Actually Good for Premium Sellers

If you’ve been watching your portfolio bleed this quarter, here’s the silver lining: elevated implied volatility means fatter premiums. When fear drives IV higher, options sellers get paid more to take on risk — and right now, several quality names are sitting at historically high IVR readings while their underlying businesses remain fundamentally sound.

I ran a quantitative analysis across seven tickers on my radar — INTU, UBER, ADBE, CMCSA, OKTA, QLYS, and DXC — scoring each across four dimensions: options liquidity, fundamental quality, IV suitability, and strategy compatibility. Here’s what I found, and which names made it onto my April watchlist for new positions.

The Scoring Framework

Each ticker was scored out of 10 across four weighted categories:

Category Weight What it measures
Options liquidity 3 pts Open interest, bid/ask spreads, weekly availability
Fundamentals 3 pts FCF, moat, profitability, balance sheet
IV suitability 2 pts IVR vs. own history, beta-adjusted premium
Strategy compatibility 2 pts How many strategies are realistically viable

Higher score = more strategies available, better risk/reward, more confidence in assignment.

🟢 Tier 1 — My Active April Watchlist

These are the names I’m actively looking to open positions on. Strong fundamentals, excellent options liquidity, and IV environments that justify selling premium right now.

#1 — INTU (Intuit Inc) · Score: 8.5/10

The best all-rounder on the list. Intuit is a low-beta (0.56) SaaS powerhouse — QuickBooks dominates SMB accounting, TurboTax owns consumer tax filing, and the data network effects create real switching costs. At ~$400, it’s sitting below both its 50d and 200d moving averages, which means elevated IVR (~60%) and juicy premium relative to its own history.

Key metrics: - Market cap: $115B · FCF: ~$3.5B · Beta: 0.56 - IV estimate: 32–38% · IVR estimate: ~60% - 52-week range: $310–$540 · Earnings: May 21 ⚠️ - Options liquidity: Excellent (weekly + monthly + liquid LEAPS)

What makes it special: Every strategy on the board works here — CSP, Wheel, Covered Call, PMCC, and Put Spreads. That flexibility is rare. Low beta means assignment won’t destroy you if the market drops another 10%.

Strategy Sell $370 Put
Expiry May 16, 2026
Est. premium ~$6–8 credit
Capital required ~$37,000
Return / 40 days ~1.6–2.2%
Annualized yield ~15–20%

Breakeven ~$363–364

⚠️ Earnings May 21 — close or roll all positions before that date. INTU historically moves ±8–12% on earnings day.

Wheel path: If assigned at $370, sell the June $400 covered call for an additional 2–3% return.

PMCC path: Buy Jan 2027 $340 LEAPS, sell monthly calls against them for capital-efficient exposure.

#2 — UBER (Uber Technologies) · Score: 8.0/10

The highest-premium name on the list. UBER sits at ~$70, down 31% from its 52-week high of $102, with IV running at 40–48% and IVR around 65%. That’s a lot of fear priced in for a company generating $6.8B in free cash flow. The 52-week low sits at $60.63 — a natural support level that makes the $63 put strike interesting.

Key metrics: - Market cap: $150B · FCF: ~$6.8B · Beta: ~1.40 - IV estimate: 40–48% · IVR estimate: ~65% - 52-week range: $60–$102 · Avg volume: 15M+ daily - Options liquidity: Excellent (massive OI, tight spreads)

The catch: Beta of 1.4 means this name moves hard with the broader market. Size your position accordingly — I’d cap UBER at 50% of what I’d put into INTU.

📌 Trade Example — Cash Secured Put
Strategy Sell $63 Put
Expiry May 16, 2026
Est. premium ~$1.80–2.20 credit
Return / 40 days ~2.8–3.5%
Annualized yield ~25–32%
Probability of profit ~72%
Breakeven ~$61.00

Put spread alternative: Sell $63P / Buy $58P for defined risk if you don’t want full assignment exposure. Sacrifices some premium but caps your max loss.

🟡 Tier 2 — Selective Entries

Quality names, but either IV environment, trend, or structural concerns require more careful positioning. Worth watching, but I’m being selective here.

#3 — ADBE (Adobe Inc) · Score: 7.5/10

Adobe is a paradox: exceptional fundamentals, challenging trend. At ~$242, it’s down 43% from its 52-week high of $423 and trading near its 52-week low of $233. The $8B FCF and 35% ROE are best-in-class — but the AI commoditization narrative (generative image tools competing with Firefly) has been a persistent headwind.

Key metrics: - Market cap: $107B · FCF: ~$8.0B · ROE: ~35% - IV estimate: 42–52% · IVR estimate: ~70% (highest in tier 1/2) - 52-week range: $233–$423 · Beta: ~1.10

My approach: Defined-risk spreads only on ADBE. Do not sell naked puts here — if support at $233 breaks, there’s a lot of air below. The put spread limits your max loss while still capturing elevated premium.

Strategy Sell $220P / Buy $210P
Expiry May 16, 2026
Est. net credit ~$1.80–2.50
Max risk $7.50–8.20 per spread
Return on risk ~22–30%
Probability of profit ~75%

PMCC alternative: Jan 2027 $200 LEAPS + sell monthly calls. Capital-efficient way to participate if you’re bullish on Adobe’s AI pivot without full downside exposure.

#4 — CMCSA (Comcast Corp) · Score: 6.5/10

The defensive income name of the group. CMCSA trades at ~$30 with a 3.4% dividend yield, 7.5x P/E, and $14B FCF. Low IV (25–30%) means per-contract premium is modest, but you’re stacking covered call income on top of a dividend — total yield potential of 15–19% annualized.

Key metrics: - Market cap: $120B · FCF: ~$14B · Dividend: ~3.4% - IV estimate: 25–30% · IVR estimate: ~45% - P/E: ~7.5x · Beta: ~0.75

The play: Buy shares → sell covered calls above current price → collect dividend. Simple Wheel setup for conservative income.

⚠️ Q1 earnings due April 23 — close all options positions before that date.

🔴 Tier 3 — Approach with Caution (or Skip)

#5 — OKTA (Okta Inc) · Score: 6.0/10

High premium, real risk. OKTA has the second-highest IVR (~68%) in the group — the market is pricing in a lot of uncertainty for the identity security leader. The problem: it’s not yet GAAP profitable, it’s in a strong downtrend from $128, and AI-native alternatives are pressuring its competitive narrative.

My approach: Put spreads only, never uncovered puts. If you’re bullish on the AI-identity security thesis long-term, a deeply OTM CSP at $65–68 with aggressive sizing discipline could work. Keep allocation small.

#6 — QLYS (Qualys Inc) · Score: 5.5/10

QLYS has the best fundamental quality in cybersecurity on this list — 45.7% operating margin, 59% YoY FCF growth, near-zero debt. The catch is thin options liquidity (~300K average daily volume). Bid/ask spreads run $0.50–1.00+ wide, which means you’ll give up significant edge on fills.

Rule: Always use limit orders at the midpoint. Monthly expirations only. Keep position size small.

❌ DXC — Do Not Trade

DXC Technology scores 2.5/10 for one simple reason: extreme IV on a distressed stock is not an opportunity, it’s a warning.

  • Down ~84% from its all-time high of $92
  • Declining revenue, ongoing restructuring, D/E ratio of 1.80
  • Options: poor liquidity, wide spreads, minimal open interest
  • If assigned at any strike, the stock could continue falling well below your cost basis

The rule of thumb: When IV is extreme because the underlying is in structural distress, you’re not getting paid for volatility — you’re getting paid to take on credit risk. Skip this one entirely.

Final Thoughts

The selloff has done something useful: it’s pushed IV higher on names with strong underlying businesses. That’s the setup premium sellers live for. INTU and UBER are the clearest opportunities right now — liquid markets, elevated IV, and fundamentals that make assignment manageable.

ADBE is compelling on paper but needs to be handled carefully given the proximity to its 52-week low. Use spreads, not naked exposure.

And DXC? Extreme IV is tempting, but distress-driven volatility is a trap. Walk away.

As always — size positions conservatively, confirm live chain data before entering, and have a plan for what you’ll do if you get assigned.
